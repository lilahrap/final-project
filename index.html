For NYC Public Employees, the Gender Paygap is Much Smaller Than You'd Think

On average in total, women make $.90 to men's dollar

In 2013, for salaried full-time New York City public employees, women made on average 10% less than men overall. This nearly matches the 11% pay gap in the federal government, and is significantly better than the national average pay gap of 23%. Check out the chart below: in some departments, like Corrections and Homeless Services, women actually make more on average than men.
