For NYC Public Employees, A Woman makes $.90 to a Man's Dollar

Here's a breakdown of the gender pay gap for New York's top 20 biggest city departments

In 2013, for salaried full-time New York City public employees, women made on average 10% less than men overall. This nearly matches the 11% pay gap in the federal government, and is significantly better than the national average pay gap of 23%.
